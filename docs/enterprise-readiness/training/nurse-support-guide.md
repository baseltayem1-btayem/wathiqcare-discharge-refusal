# Nurse / Support Staff Guide — Clinical Workspace 2.0

## Your role

You help patients use the patient-facing consent journey on a tablet or their own device. You do not sign the consent unless you are acting as a witness.

---

## Before the patient starts

1. Confirm the physician has sent the consent.
2. Open the secure signing link or the **Preview patient journey** in Clinical Workspace 2.0.
3. Ensure the device is charged and connected.

## Helping the patient

1. **Language**
   - Ask the patient which language they prefer.
   - Tap **English** or **العربية** at the top of any patient screen.
2. **Text size**
   - If the patient has difficulty reading, change **Text size** to Large or Extra large.
3. **High contrast**
   - Tap **High contrast** if it helps the patient see the screen.

## Walking through the journey

1. **Landing**
   - Confirm the patient name, procedure, physician, and facility.
   - Tap **Start reviewing**.
2. **Education**
   - Let the patient read each tab at their own pace.
   - Answer basic navigation questions only; clinical questions must go to the physician.
   - In **Understanding**, the patient must answer the comprehension questions.
3. **Questions**
   - If the patient has a question, help them type it and tap **Send question**.
   - Inform the physician if a response is needed before signing.
4. **Decision**
   - The patient taps **I Accept** or **I Refuse**.
   - If refusing, the patient must acknowledge consequences and sign the refusal.
5. **Signature**
   - The patient taps **Verify OTP** (simulated in the prototype).
   - The patient reviews their full name and taps **Sign consent**.
6. **Confirmation**
   - Show the patient the confirmation screen with the reference and timestamp.

## If additional participants are required

| Participant | When needed | What to do |
|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Guardian | Patient is a minor or lacks capacity | Complete guardian attestation before patient signs. |
| Interpreter | Patient prefers a language other than Arabic | Complete interpreter attestation before patient signs. |
| Witness | Procedure is high-risk or uses anesthesia | Have a witness sign before the patient signs. |

## After signing

- Confirm the timeline shows the signature event.
- Do not close the browser until the confirmation screen appears.
- Report any errors to the physician or IT support.

---

## Accessibility reminders

- Offer to change language, text size, or contrast before starting.
- Speak slowly and avoid rushing the patient.
- Do not answer clinical questions; record them for the physician.
