# PATIENT USER GUIDE — Mobile Consent Signing

**Audience:** Patients receiving an informed consent link from their physician.
**Release:** v1.0.1
**Languages:** Arabic (primary), English.

---

## 1. What you will receive

Your physician will send you a secure link by SMS. The message looks similar to:

> WathiqCare: Your physician has shared an informed consent for your upcoming procedure. Please review and respond: `https://wathiqcare.online/sign/<secure-code>/workflow`

Open the link on the **same mobile phone** that received the SMS. The link is private to you and expires after a set period.

---

## 2. What you will see — step by step

A progress indicator at the top of the screen will show **Step N of M** so you always know where you are.

### Step 1 — Educational Materials
- Read the information your physician prepared about your procedure.
- Includes purpose, risks, benefits, alternatives, and what to expect.
- Some materials may include images or short videos.
- You can scroll back to re-read any section before continuing.

### Step 2 — Consent Review
- A plain-language summary of the consent document.
- A preview of the consent PDF you will be signing.
- Two buttons appear at the bottom:
  - **Accept and Continue** — you agree to proceed.
  - **Refuse Treatment** — you decline the procedure.

### Step 3a — If you Accepted: OTP Verification
- Enter the mobile number where you want to receive a one-time code.
- Tap **Request OTP**. You will see the masked phone number where the code was sent and the delivery status.
- Enter the 6-digit code from the SMS.
- Tap **Verify OTP**.

### Step 3b — If you Refused: Refusal Acknowledgement
- Read the acknowledgement that confirms you understand the consequences of refusal.
- Confirm the acknowledgement to proceed.
- You will then be asked to sign the **Refusal Signature**, which is captured the same way as a normal signature (Step 4).

### Step 4 — Signature
- Sign with your finger or stylus in the signature pad on the screen.
- Tap **Clear** to redraw if you are not satisfied.
- Tap **Submit Signature** to finalize.

### Step 5 — Confirmation
- You will see a confirmation screen with a reference number.
- A copy of the signed (or refused) consent is preserved in your medical record.

---

## 3. OTP — Frequently asked

- **I did not receive the SMS.** Wait up to 60 seconds. If still nothing, tap **Request OTP** again. Confirm the mobile number is correct.
- **The code expired.** Request a new code. Old codes stop working as soon as a new one is issued.
- **I entered the wrong code.** You can retry. Repeated wrong attempts will lock OTP for a short cool-down period — see Section 5.
- **Why do I need OTP?** It proves that the person signing controls the phone number on record. This protects you legally.

---

## 4. Consent review — what to do

- Read every section, including risks.
- If you have any question, contact your physician **before** tapping **Accept and Continue**.
- You are not committed until you complete Step 4 (Signature).
- If you change your mind during Step 3 or Step 4, simply close the browser. Nothing is finalized until you submit your signature.

---

## 5. Troubleshooting

| Symptom | What to do |
|---|---|
| Link does not open | Confirm you have an internet connection. Tap the link again from the original SMS. |
| "Link expired" message | Contact your physician's office to request a new link. |
| OTP SMS not arriving | Wait 60s, then tap **Request OTP** again. Confirm the mobile number. If still no SMS after two attempts, contact your physician's office. |
| OTP code "invalid" | Re-check the digits. Codes are 6 digits and time-limited. |
| OTP locked / cool-down | Wait the displayed cool-down period and try again. |
| Signature pad will not capture | Use your finger directly on the screen. If using a stylus, ensure it is recognized by your device. Try again after refreshing. |
| Screen frozen during signature submit | Do not close the page. Wait up to 60 seconds. If still frozen, refresh and re-open the link — your previous progress through OTP is preserved within the session window. |
| Wrong language | Use the language toggle at the top-right of the page. |
| Accessibility — text too small | Use your phone's built-in zoom or accessibility text-scaling. |
| I no longer want to sign | Tap **Refuse Treatment** in Step 2, or simply close the browser before submitting Step 4. |

If you encounter any other issue, contact your physician's office and quote the **reference number** shown on screen (if available).

---

## 6. Privacy assurance

- Your phone number is masked everywhere it is displayed back to you.
- Only your treating physician and authorized clinical/legal personnel may access the consent record.
- Your data is handled in accordance with the Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL).
