# WathiqCare Enterprise UX 2.0 — Final Roadmap

**Principal Product Designer | WathiqCare Enterprise Edition**

---

## Overview

This roadmap consolidates all work streams from the WathiqCare Enterprise UX 2.0 masterplan into a phased, measurable delivery plan. It is designed to bring the product from its current prototype-heavy state to a world-class enterprise healthcare experience.

---

## Strategic Milestones

| Milestone | Target | Definition of Done |
|---|---|---|
| **M1: Foundation** | Week 4 | Design system tokens, IA, and base components established. |
| **M2: Physician Experience** | Week 11 | Canonical physician workspace redesigned and prototyped. |
| **M3: Patient Experience** | Week 15 | Patient signing journey redesigned and prototyped. |
| **M4: Enterprise Surfaces** | Week 18 | Compliance and admin surfaces redesigned. |
| **M5: Pilot Readiness** | Week 22 | UX validated, accessible, performant, and ready for internal pilot. |
| **M6: General Availability** | Week 30 | Full rollout with documentation, training, and support. |

---

## Phase 1: Discovery & Foundation (Weeks 1–4)

### Goals
- Understand current state and user needs.
- Establish design system foundations and information architecture.

### Activities
- Audit existing UI, screenshots, and prototypes.
- Conduct stakeholder interviews with physicians, nurses, compliance, and patients.
- Define design principles and success metrics.
- Build Figma foundations: color, typography, spacing, icons.
- Publish base component library.

### Deliverables
- `01-product-vision.md`
- `02-design-principles.md`
- `03-information-architecture.md`
- `04-design-system.md`
- Figma foundation libraries.

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Stakeholders align on vision and principles.
- [ ] Design system tokens are ready for engineering.
- [ ] IA map covers all primary tasks.

---

## Phase 2: Physician Workspace (Weeks 5–11)

### Goals
- Design and prototype the canonical physician consent workflow.

### Activities
- Consolidate competing physician surfaces.
- Design three-pane workspace layout.
- Build consent task flow components.
- Create interactive prototype for usability testing.
- Produce desktop, tablet, and RTL variants.

### Deliverables
- `05-physician-workspace.md`
- Figma physician workspace screens.
- Clickable prototype.
- Usability test plan.

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Standard consent task completable in ≤ 3 minutes in prototype.
- [ ] Clinical safety review passed.
- [ ] Physicians rate ease of use ≥ 4/5 in testing.

---

## Phase 3: Patient Journey (Weeks 12–15)

### Goals
- Design and prototype the patient signing experience.

### Activities
- Define canonical patient signing route and flow.
- Design mobile-first education, decision, signature, and confirmation screens.
- Produce Arabic RTL variants.
- Validate plain-language content with health literacy review.

### Deliverables
- `06-patient-journey.md`
- Figma patient journey screens.
- Clickable prototype.
- Content review sign-off.

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Standard patient journey completable in ≤ 5 minutes in prototype.
- [ ] Patient comprehension score ≥ 80%.
- [ ] Decline flow is respectful and complete.

---

## Phase 4: Component Library & Mobile (Weeks 16–18)

### Goals
- Complete the component library and mobile strategy.

### Activities
- Build remaining consent-specific components.
- Define responsive behavior for all key screens.
- Design tablet bedside and kiosk modes.
- Document component usage and accessibility.

### Deliverables
- `07-component-library.md`
- `08-mobile-strategy.md`
- Complete Figma component library.
- Mobile and tablet screen variants.

### Success Criteria
- [ ] All components have accessible states.
- [ ] Mobile screens cover all primary tasks.
- [ ] Component library is engineering-ready.

---

## Phase 5: Accessibility & Compliance Surfaces (Weeks 19–22)

### Goals
- Ensure accessibility and complete enterprise surfaces.

### Activities
- Conduct WCAG 2.2 AA audit.
- Remediate accessibility issues.
- Design compliance dashboard and admin console.
- Build reports and data visualizations.
- Test with assistive technologies.

### Deliverables
- `09-accessibility.md`
- Compliance and admin Figma screens.
- Accessibility audit report.
- Remediation plan.

### Success Criteria
- [ ] No critical or high accessibility violations.
- [ ] Compliance screens support real audit workflows.
- [ ] Assistive technology testing passed.

---

## Phase 6: Implementation Support & Pilot (Weeks 23–26)

### Goals
- Support engineering implementation and prepare for pilot.

### Activities
- Provide redline specs and design annotations.
- Conduct design QA during implementation.
- Iterate based on engineering feedback.
- Prepare pilot training materials.
- Define pilot success metrics and feedback loops.

### Deliverables
- Engineering handoff package.
- Design QA checklist.
- Pilot training deck.
- Pilot feedback template.

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Engineering handoff complete.
- [ ] Design QA issues resolved.
- [ ] Pilot users trained.

---

## Phase 7: Validation & General Availability (Weeks 27–30)

### Goals
- Validate with real users and release to general availability.

### Activities
- Run internal IMC pilot.
- Collect and analyze feedback.
- Iterate on high-priority issues.
- Finalize documentation and support materials.
- Launch GA.

### Deliverables
- Pilot report.
- Iteration backlog.
- GA release notes.
- User guides for physicians, patients, and admins.

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Pilot completion rate ≥ 85%.
- [ ] Physician and patient satisfaction ≥ 4/5.
- [ ] No critical defects at GA.
