# Patient Explanation — Informed Consent with WathiqCare

## What is informed consent?

Informed consent means you understand the procedure your care team recommends, including the benefits, risks, and alternatives, and you agree to it freely.

---

## What you will do

1. **Review your information**
   - Confirm your name, the procedure, your physician, and the facility.
2. **Learn about the procedure**
   - Read a summary, risks, benefits, and instructions.
   - Watch or read any education material your physician has shared.
   - Answer a few questions to check your understanding.
3. **Ask questions**
   - If anything is unclear, type a question for your care team.
4. **Make your decision**
   - You can tap **I Accept** or **I Refuse**.
   - Your care will not be affected if you refuse.
   - You can change your mind before the procedure begins.
5. **Sign**
   - Verify your identity with the code sent to your mobile.
   - Review your name and sign.

---

## Your rights

- You have the right to information in a language you understand.
- You have the right to ask questions until you are satisfied.
- You have the right to refuse without losing care or respect.
- You have the right to have a guardian, interpreter, or witness present if needed.

---

## If you need help

- Ask a nurse or support staff member to change the language, text size, or contrast.
- Ask them to help you type a question.
- Do not sign until you feel you understand.

---

## After you sign

- You will see a confirmation screen with a reference number and time.
- A copy is saved to your clinical record.
- You can ask for a patient copy.
